About Us – SELBYME

SELBYME is driven by a mission to transform everyday shopping for millions of Indians. Built on four core principles — customer obsession over competitor focus, passion for innovation, commitment to operational excellence, and long-term vision — we aim to become India’s most customer-centric company, India’s best employer, and India’s safest place to work.

At SELBYME, we believe shopping should be simple, fast, and affordable. That’s why we connect local stores and sellers directly to customers, ensuring on-demand delivery of groceries, household items, dairy products, and more — at local prices, with doorstep convenience. We’re not just building an e-commerce platform; we’re creating a movement to empower small towns, villages, and everyday entrepreneurs across India.

We offer two delivery options:

  • 15-minute priority delivery (₹9)
  • 4-hour free delivery

We also reward customers who return packaging, promote low prices with quality service, and operate with transparency and accountability. Our model combines the best of Amazon, Blinkit, and local retail — rooted in trust, community, and innovation.

Our Leadership – Lavkesh Sharma, Founder & CEO

Lavkesh Sharma, the visionary behind SELBYME, started the company with a bicycle, a ₹4,000 investment, and a dream — to make essential goods accessible to every Indian, regardless of location or income level. Now, SELBYME stands as a ₹1 crore valuation company.

Raised in a lower-middle-class family, Lavkesh personally understands the challenges faced by everyday consumers and small business owners. His leadership principles are practical and grounded:

  • Serve the customer like family.
  • Act fast, stay humble.
  • Hire local, dream national.
  • Innovate daily with limited resources.
  • Win trust through action.
“I don’t want to be seen as the founder. I’m a delivery boy of a mission — to bring better service, pricing, and respect to every Indian doorstep.”
— Lavkesh Sharma

Join us in building the future of Indian commerce — one order, one village, and one customer at a time.
